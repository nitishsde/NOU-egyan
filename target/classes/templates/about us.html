ABOUT US

The Nalanda Open University is the only University in the State of Bihar meant for imparting learning exclusively through the system of distance education. The University was established in March, 1987 by an ordinance, promulgated by the Government of Bihar. Later, Nalanda Open University Act, 1995 was passed by the Bihar Legislature, replacing the Ordinance, and the University came under the authority and jurisdiction of the new Act automatically. The University is named after the famous Nalanda University of Ancient India. At present, the University is functioning from its camp office at Biscomaun Bhawan, 2nd, 3rd, 4th and 12th Floors, Patna – 800001.

The University is recognised by the Distance Education Council (DEC), University Grants Commission, and Ministry of HRD, Government of India for imparting education through distance mode. Its objective is to provide educational opportunities to those who are unable to take up formal education and still desire to upgrade their qualifications through correspondence courses, contact programmes, study centres and mass media.

The University also aims to create awareness for self-sufficiency, equip people with knowledge and higher qualification, and provide courses for rural, agricultural, industrial and commercial needs of people.

History Background

Nalanda, located near present day Patna, was one of the world’s greatest ancient centres of learning. The famous Chinese scholar Hiuen-Tsang studied here and described its grandeur. Excavations reveal monasteries, meditation halls, lecture halls and libraries that show Nalanda was once a global centre of serious study.

Buddhist and Jain texts describe Nalanda as a place visited by Lord Buddha and Mahavira. Emperor Ashoka erected a temple here, and Nagarjuna, the great Mahayana philosopher, studied and taught at Nalanda. The revival of Nalanda through Nalanda Open University continues this ancient legacy of knowledge and learning.
